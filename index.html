About Baltimore's Veg Scene

bmoreveg is a curated guide to 21 vegetarian, vegan, and veg-friendly restaurants, cafes, and bakeries across 13 Baltimore-area neighborhoods. We cover cuisines from South Indian and Sichuan to Georgian, Turkish, Mexican, Italian, and Asian fusion — every spot selected for quality, character, and kitchens that genuinely know their way around vegetables.

Not every spot is strictly vegan or vegetarian, but every place here is somewhere a plant-forward diner can eat well and feel good about it. Baltimore's food culture is unpretentious and real. This guide reflects that.

Featured neighborhoods include Hampden, Remington, Canton, Fells Point, Mt. Vernon, Downtown, Station North, Charles Village, Roland Park, Parkville, Timonium, Columbia, and Reisterstown.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Baltimore good for vegans and vegetarians?

Yes. Baltimore has a growing and diverse veg-friendly dining scene. While it may not have as many fully vegan restaurants as larger cities, most neighborhoods have at least one strong option for plant-forward diners. Cuisines range from South Indian and Sichuan to Turkish, Georgian, and Mexican, and many restaurants offer dedicated vegetarian and vegan menus rather than afterthought options.

What are the best vegan restaurants in Baltimore?

Top vegan picks include Oleum in Fells Point (a fully vegan restaurant with globally inspired dishes like rocket soup and house-made tiramisu), Pavan Foods in Parkville (all-vegetarian South Indian with samosa chaat and masala dosa), and Toki Underground in Station North (creative vegan ramen and cauliflower bao buns). See our full vegan guide.

Where can I get vegan soul food in Baltimore?

While bmoreveg focuses on veg-friendly restaurants across many cuisines, Baltimore's vegan soul food scene is still developing. For plant-based comfort food with bold flavors, try Ekiben's Taiwanese-Ethiopian fusion bowls in Hampden (tofu nuggets, tempura broccoli, steamed buns) or Oleum's reimagined comfort classics in Fells Point.

What are the best vegetarian Indian restaurants near Baltimore?

The Baltimore area has excellent Indian options for vegetarians. Pavan Foods in Parkville is entirely vegetarian with outstanding South Indian dishes. Namaste in Roland Park is open 365 days a year with extensive vegan options and great momo dumplings. Sangam north of Timonium specializes in crispy dosas. For special occasions, Royal Taj and Ananda in Columbia offer elevated Indian fine dining. See our full Indian restaurant guide.

Are there any fully vegan restaurants in Baltimore?

Yes. Oleum at 701 S Bond St in Fells Point is a fully vegan restaurant serving upscale, globally inspired cuisine. Their entire menu is plant-based, including house-made cheeses, sausages, and desserts. Pavan Foods in Parkville is entirely vegetarian and most dishes are fully vegan.

Where is the best vegan brunch in Baltimore?

Oleum offers weekend brunch with vegan options. For bakery-style brunch, Café Dear Leon in Canton and La Maison in Remington serve outstanding pastries (many vegan-adaptable). Artifact Coffee in Hampden is a spacious warehouse café with donuts and outdoor seating. See our full brunch guide.

What neighborhoods in Baltimore have the most veg-friendly restaurants?

Hampden, Remington, and Canton each have three or more veg-friendly spots within walking distance. Fells Point has Baltimore's only fully vegan restaurant (Oleum) plus multiple other options. Columbia offers two elevated Indian restaurants. For a full neighborhood breakdown, browse our interactive map or filter by neighborhood above.
